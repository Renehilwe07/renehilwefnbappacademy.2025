ELEPHANT

Elephants, the largest land mammals, are known for their size, intelligence, and complex social structures. There are two main species: African elephants (savanna and forest) and Asian elephants. Elephants have a unique anatomy, including a trunk used for a variety of functions, large ears for heat regulation, and tusks that serve multiple purposes.

Key Features: Size and Weight: African elephants are the largest land animals, with males reaching up to 3 meters in height and weighing up to 6,000 kg. Trunk: The trunk is a highly sensitive organ used for smelling, breathing, drinking, feeding, and more. Ears: Large ears help elephants dissipate heat. Tusks: Tusks, which are modified incisor teeth, are used for digging, stripping bark, fighting, and defense. Intelligence and Social Behavior: Elephants are highly intelligent and have complex social structures, including matriarchal family units. Lifespan: Elephants can live for 60-70 years in the wild, according to www.wwf.org.uk. Habitat: African elephants are found in Africa, while Asian elephants are found in South and Southeast Asia.
